Childhood memories

Mar 15, 2016

Sometimes I’ll get caught up in a good memory
From when I was a young kid.
Not unlike a dream, many details cloudy yet vivid.
But as if the wrong note was struck on a piano mid-progression,
A color takes over the dream.

I’ll remember the circumstances surrounding the good
And shame poisons the moment.
You weren’t giving me an allowance,
You were bribing me.
You weren’t asking me to join you up north to see me more,
But to make her see me less.

Most of my life I looked at as as normal of an upbringing as one could have,
But the reality was that I made do with what I was given.
Given your circumstances, people will paraphrase,
You’re really quite well-adjusted.

Realistically though
I’m just coming to terms with damage that’s long been done.
Over and over.
For years and years.
I will lay in bed at night
Terrified for my past self,
Wishing I could be there to comfort him,
Or to pull him out of the situations he was in.

There is a trite saying: nobody’s perfect.
Everybody seemed to refer to that as I grew up.
Whoever shrieked the most violently won.
Whoever grappled and neutralized the other fastest won.

My room was my only safe place.
A small place with all of my things,
All of my distractions.
Loud things that could drown out a cacophony of anger outside.

A dismissive air afterwards where we act like nothing happened.
We sit down to dinner and look at our food
And the adults smile and force small talk.
You just got finished calling him a worthless vulture,
My mashed potatoes are fine.
I heard the sharp echoes of your psychotic outburst.
The walls aren’t that thick.
Don’t pretend like the spaghetti is the only thing going on.
I wonder if it was the alcohol that brought this out in you
Or if it’s being sober and paranoid of it that keeps it in.

Mom and Dad aren’t so different behind it all.
And how far from the tree does the apple typically fall?
