Screen to screen

May 26, 2016

She put down her phone
She was done with it
She felt too connected, always plugged in
The glow of screen on her face was diseasing
The attention it demanded was unceasing
Unbothered, the rest of her friends stayed connected
A widening circle of new activities
Last-minute invitations to last-minute parties
Trips planned entirely using thumbs and vibrations
They traveled the world together and others still separate
Viewing through their lens their friendsâ€™ adventures
A new story to prod when they returned or passed through
The glow of their shares between them grew warmer
But she missed it all while she was sitting at home
Her departure forgotten as the community evolved
The glow of her TV instead filled her days
While the world carried on without her, unfazed
