The Dating Factory

May 10, 2016

It takes a long time
To get up to speed
Lots of things to do
Before the business springs afloat
Not just the haircuts and about me's
Not just the photos of being interesting
But eventually, after all of the prep
The factory is open

When the doors swing wide
There's a hum through the place
A toe tapping ticking without patrons swinging by
Tending to the pistons, being sure it's up to snuff
I always think the work is done enough
A trickle drips through
And the leaves an echo in the warehouse
An antsy tender tends to them
In hopes they are a keeper
But since they'd just stumbled in
Not expecting much
They're scared away before there is a chance for "stay in touch!"
The hum turns to a whistle
As the wind blows through the shop
The factory is running
But producing like it's stopped.

Then one day it's come alive
Swarmed with peekers peeking everywhere
And askers asking me when I want to get together
The change is so abrupt that I don't know how to handle
The juggling act before me, throwing more and more amidst them
Running full capacity is a fun exhausting war
But then I find I've had enough and can't handle any more
The lever shudders shut
And soon so do the doors
I rush the remainders out
Awkwardly and wincing
I tell them the straight truth
But I fear I'm unconvincing
But when they're finally gone
I sigh and droop my head
I'm so damn excited now
For time alone in my bed
