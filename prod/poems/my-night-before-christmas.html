My night before Christmas

Dec 24, 2014

'Twas the night before Christmas
And all through the streets
Not a creature was stirring
Except for, of course, me

After learning a trick
From which skills I could grow
A hiccup turned shin slice
And I outcried, “oh no”

My insurance was so bad
That I looked overseas
I could travel to France
For ER to stitch this knee

So I waited it out
Hoping assistance I'd need not
But by 3am still
The blood would not clot

And out from eureka
An urgent care shone
Open 365/7/24
Where I would drive out alone

It was expectedly quiet
With the receptionist's greeting
Asking if I needed pain meds
“No, it just won't stop bleeding”

The doc and the nurse
Caught me looking around
In their own tired haste
Told me to put on the gown

I chatted and joked
As they prepared their skin tools
They suggested I looked away
I said, “no this will be cool”

The mending was quick
Finishing just before dawn
They found it impressive
How, unfazed, I'd looked on

I thanked them profusely
In my loud, exiting finale
Didn't think I'd spend Christmas
Down here in Apple Valley
