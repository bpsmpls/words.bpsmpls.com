Belle Isle, Richmond

Apr 02, 2021

A loose mission:
Explore the James River MTB trails.

Straight south through campus,
Down many stairs,
Bopping sidewalk roots,
Now-familiar jibs of mine feel,
When watching Kate's handling excel, mild.

An under-bridge bridge found to cross.
Underfoot a spectacle of hurtling rapids.
This gateway to some mapped blue squiggles,
That encircle this section of river.
Starting with some abandoned buildings and
An MTB skills park on Belle Isle.

Old ruins lining leafy hallways
Littered with quiet polite folks.
Many who will start up with us excited to share trail conditions
Or their very best spots.
Still we stray a bit,
Set our bikes aside,
And scramble on slabs
Along the riverside's swell and smile.

Early week's rains behind us
Left tacky dirt along these ridges
Yet by now we had to raincheck this trail ride.
The sunset's lowering creep reminds us
This trail could take, far as we could tell, a while.

Back over another low bridge,
Through Civil War memorial parking lots,
Homeward bound but gassed
To pedal a hill-from-Hell trial.

Pizza, arancini, and a sister waited at home.
And we were hungry and tired.
Though of all the goofy tricks,
Meandering,
Discovering,
Backtracking,
And sessioning,
We didn't even pedal twelve miles.
