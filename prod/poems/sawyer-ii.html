Sawyer II

May 17, 2016

Open with a joke just to see where it’ll land
But missing gear, it couldn’t safely roll in
On approach, the joke tipped to the right a bit hard
And burned fast in the crash greeting it.
She told him she was being sarcastic
But he didn’t think she knew what that meant
She told him he didn’t get her humor
Boy, did he agree

Because she chose what to see and projected what she liked
Her disappointment inside her grew
And if every man she laid in bed with understood her immediately
There’d only have been just a few

She’s conflating a kindness with knowing
He assumed she was wiser than he
Her desire to be close and desired
Manifested by pushing him away

It hadn’t been that long by then yet they were drained
By every conversation they could have
Confused and comparing to what they’d expected
How could it have unraveled so fast?

She could feel it, and so could he.
Every sentence between them was a chore
They released the grasp that had been slipping
Agreeing together that it wasn’t worth it anymore
