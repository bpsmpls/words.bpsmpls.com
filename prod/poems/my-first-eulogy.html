My first eulogy

Sep 12, 2009

It may be 6 a.m. somewhere
But it's one o'clock here
I can't stop thinking
I can't stop remembering
You've already been gone
For a lifetime
And the one that you filled
At its end
In the box
Amounts to a life threefold.

You never knew how much pain you could cause.
A life spent in misery
Bringing happiness around with him
Instead of inside of him.
A life spent in experience
Not in slavery to the status quo
Hard pressed into literature
Of outstanding magnitude,
While in a train car
Filled with the homeless
Along the landscape
Of the entire country
Thinking only
How much he loves the freedom.

A life spent without
The American dream,
Rather stretched into
The American Imagination.
If all substance is formed of strings
And all life supported by selfishness
And all selfishness dependent on survival
You depend on your seclusion to be your downfall.
Survival Destructo.

The broken man
Free from everything but himself
Rode his dirty horse
Around the ranch.
Stopping only for a few
Precious moments.
Precision marked by a perfect once-over.
Decisions marked by a curious understanding.
Conversation marked by a responsive listener.
Altercations transition a friendship's development.
The tired man
Wasting time trying to sleep
Dives beneath the surface.
Pushing downward and forward.
Hiding more from others,
Pushing more on himself.
The distant man
Looking for something new
Finds a gem in the sand
Almost every time
He looks around.

The independence,
The sacrifice,
The space,
The love
The hate
And everything,
Falls under an Earley gene.
They scrounge for a bit of experience
They dive into wombs of unfamiliarity.
They think with their hearts,
But decide with their mind.

A soulful man
Ends his time
Facing a sign
That leaves his note.
The sign reads
Dead end
He hopes they'll know what he means
He hopes that he will be missed
He hopes that it frees him
He hopes that it's right
He says that some will get it.
He says that he's forgotten
He says his prison's opening
By the work to his left
That pours his peace into the car
He closes his eyes
His face washing from angst
Underneath the water
Underneath his foam
The serene emerges
In a tearful goodbye
Alone in the night
He sings to himself:

*{
Everything comes together again
Does this pale figure offend
Show me the way under your skin
Don’t inhale, don’t excite
Maybe the dark is really the light
Covered in shame, dirt and a dress
Not any good, not any bad, just give it a rest
Dug his own grave with the choices he made now he’s dead
Nothing has changed it’s still the same was he ever alive
I’ve said it before I’ll say it again I’m tired of this
I don’t think about it
I don’t talk about it
I don’t dream about it
Not anymore
}

*{Excerpted from "Am I Done Yet (Almost)" by Def & Dum}
