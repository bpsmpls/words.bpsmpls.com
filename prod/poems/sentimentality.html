Sentimentality

Sep 18, 2014

Sentimentality.
I used to cherish it.

It’s so fragile.
And it makes you so fragile.
It’s a vulnerability magnifier.

You pour uncontrolled feelings into a mold in your memory.
Everything sticks to it. It’s cognitive fly paper.
Attaching sounds, scents, feelings, auras, breezes, touches, pressures.
Flair to your brand new ship.
They grow and expand and overlap and blend, becoming inseparable from each other.
Inherent to each other.
Reminders waft and flash in.
A brief sigh and a smile.

And it sours.
Your uncontrolled feelings, now mold in your memory.
Everything is stuck to it.
Every noise, stink, sting, cloud, wind, poke, pressure.
Barnacles on your aging schooner.
A coating all the way around, impossible to chip off.
A drag, an anchor built in.
The boat is sinking.
Abandon ship.
The memory, adrift on the wreckage, alone.
I wonder if that monster is worth saving.

Sentimentality.
It’s the single most unrewarding thing I’ve known.
It might be a good place to shut things down.
