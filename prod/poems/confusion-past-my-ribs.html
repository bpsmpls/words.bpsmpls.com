Confusion past my ribs

Jul 12, 2014

There’s confusion past my ribs. My walls are tight. Smothered and unrested by my seat, I’m seeking distractions. This storm will pass, I repeat. There isn’t any rain, but the congestion’s in the air. Breathe in. Dizzying. Breathe out. Anxious.

I want to shed my environment, my room, my town, my job, my world, like tearing off my shirt. I want the remaining whistle of the breeze to cool my fever, and comfort my exposure.

It’s transition. It’s complacency slipped right from beneath. A domino falling just short, teasing whether it’ll offset more. Tiny nostalgia undulating along a foggy line, a well-meaning vagrant looking hopeful but directionless.

I shake my head. It’s too fresh. I’m indirect myself, carrying a bruise in a sleeve, careful not to elbow anyone while stumbling through. Breathe in. Hold it. Bump. Wince. Sigh.
