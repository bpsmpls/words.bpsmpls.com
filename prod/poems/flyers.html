Flyers

Feb 18, 2021

Walking the sidewalks of the neighboring small town
Knocking on every door along each street
Every door.

Behind oppressive fences
Under leafy archways
Atop crooked stairs
Inside manicured porches
Across long front yards
Staying off the grass
Steeling myself when dogs bark, when they charge
Rapping the glazing of storm doors
Thumping the stiles of solid ones
Careful to replace the latch on the gate when I leave

Many knocks are surprisingly answered
A wary eye ducking from behind a door
Always softening when they hear why I'm here
I have a spiel I've worked out and practiced.
They always hear me out.
They always take my flyer.
I think they can sense my conviction,
Maybe they can see an unusual courage.

It's been a week.
Many have already heard and they say so
But many still haven't.
I'm doing everything I can
And more than I knew I could.
I've run out of flyers and returned with more.
Folks who don't answer get one rolled in their door handle.
The flyer has your photo, name, and information.
In hopes it could help all of us find you, Tim.
