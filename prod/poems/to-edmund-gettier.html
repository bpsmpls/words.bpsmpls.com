To Edmund Gettier

May 24, 2016

The farmer looked distraught
When I arrived for my delivery
He asked if I’d seen a cow of his
Because he really couldn’t see.
I swiveled extending a finger
Down the road amidst some trees
“Don’t worry, she’s over there,” I said.
And the farmer sighed in relief.
He’d seen the fuzzy black and white
Among the blurs of green
And nodded as he thanked me
For sharing what I’d seen.
As I left his land by truck
And approached that group of trees
I saw the cow was not there
What we’d seen were dangling sheets.
The cow was behind some tall grass
Blocked from the farmer’s seat
She was of course still out there
Just hidden among the wheat
So when I told the farmer
That the cow was in the mead
Was I correct to say I knew that
If only accidentally?
