Elm with M

Feb 18, 2021

The air is quiet
Like the area is completely abandoned.
Just me and M.
The fresh snow drifting down from above us,
Gently compacting under our tires,
A muffled chewing sound with each pedal stroke.

It's dusk and the trail is still brighter than any dry season day.
The hardpack base is banked and scooped along the edges of the trail.
We have a firm bobsled run to bank off.
I keep chuckling loudly, to no one in particular.
I frequently contort to check on M,
Mostly to react to her playful hoots and hollers.
I almost always lose my line when untwisting forward
Laughing as I plant a foot in the high snow above the trail to keep my balance.

The trees are bare and still.
The trail is so open this time of year.
I can see everything.
Deep into the woods,
All the pristine nothingness that's usually hidden behind needles and leaves, thicket and brush.
It's oddly secluded.
Though far through the trees, I spot another rider every so often.
I wonder if they notice us,
Or if our giggles are muffled enough by these light flurries.

Families of deer crowd the edges of the trail
Like they're spectating a parade of two.
Some sneak into the action to be chased off.
Others cross and look back.
And they all eventually halt
And stiffly watch us pass.

Two-wheel drifting through corners,
My left pinky numbs first.
It suffered some ugly frostnip years back, so it always cools the quickest.
When we stop at intersections, our chats get shorter.
My chuckles stay hearty, but my face slurs all my words.
I'm slowly icing over from the neck up.
M however is radiating like a tanning bed.
She's laughing about how sweaty she is.
My fingers pressed against her face burn us both oppositely.
Her kiss on my cheek thaws half my face for a second.
We make our final descent to the trailhead.
M calls out from behind me,
"How lucky are we?"
