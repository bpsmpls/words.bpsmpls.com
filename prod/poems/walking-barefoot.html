Walking barefoot

Dec 17, 2016

It's like walking barefoot to the oceanfront. There's only a gravel road that leads there. It's low traffic and tunneled by large trees on either side. You're convinced it isn't that far of a walk, but you're not sure of how far exactly. The longer you walk, the more the groups of small sharp rocks catch your feet. You think you'll get used to it, that you'll reach a point where your feet will level off to a dull pain. At one point you think, I have to be halfway by now at least. You wonder if it'll even be worth it to swim by the time you get there. You imagine the cuts on your feet in that salty water, or even on the hot sand. Each wide turn of the road, you get to peer beyond the curtain of trees to see another bend. You stop waiting for the beach to appear and start considering if you'd gone the wrong way entirely.
