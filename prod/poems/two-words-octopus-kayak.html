Two words: octopus kayak

Jun 11, 2024

A paddle near the reef
Cures summer sun convection
Beyond the heat relief
Is its dazzling perfection

Bright wonders of the sea
Both fragile and mysterious
But today we learned there be
Some beings more mischievous  

In my narrow double kayak
We splashed along the water
But soon came under attack
Devolved to ocean fodder

The boat became unstable
Tentacles stretched and held firm
We tried but were unable
To combat its tugging squirm

Fearing harm awaited
We sought to stay protected
When the twisting escalated
It capsized, we ejected

Surfacing soon after
Disbelieving our own eyes
We coughed out nervous laughter 
Why our vessel didn't rise

A diver eventually showed
He swam up just to talk to us
He saw it all from below
The boat belonged now to an octopus

—
